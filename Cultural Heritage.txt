Cultural heritage encompasses the legacy of physical artifacts and intangible attributes inherited from past generations, maintained in the present, and bestowed for the benefit of future generations. Preservation of this heritage is vital for maintaining a society's identity, history, and continuity. Efforts to preserve cultural heritage range from safeguarding ancient monuments and artifacts to sustaining traditional practices and languages. These efforts face numerous challenges, including modernization, globalization, and conflict. Exploring the methods and significance of cultural heritage preservation helps us appreciate the intricate tapestry of human civilization and the need to protect it for posterity.
