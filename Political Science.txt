Political science explores the theory and practice of politics, governance, and public policy, while international relations focus on the interactions between nation-states and global entities. Together, they offer a comprehensive understanding of power dynamics, geopolitical strategies, and the complex web of global affairs. These fields examine the functioning of political institutions, the behavior of political actors, and the formulation of policies that shape domestic and international landscapes. By studying political science and international relations, we can better grasp the intricacies of governance, the challenges of diplomacy, and the pursuit of global stability and cooperation.
